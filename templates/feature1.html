Understanding Crop Features

Nitrogen (N)

Nitrogen is essential for plant growth as it is a major component of chlorophyll and amino acids. Adequate nitrogen improves leaf growth, while its deficiency can result in yellowing leaves and stunted growth. Excess nitrogen, however, may cause excessive vegetative growth and delayed crop maturity.

Phosphorous (P)

Phosphorous promotes root development and helps in flower and fruit production. It plays a crucial role in energy transfer and photosynthesis. Insufficient phosphorous may lead to dark green or purplish foliage and poor plant growth.

Potassium (K)

Potassium regulates various physiological processes, including water uptake, enzyme activation, and photosynthesis. It enhances drought resistance and improves overall crop quality. Deficiency can cause leaf scorch and poor resistance to pests and diseases.

Soil pH

Soil pH determines nutrient availability in the soil. Most crops prefer slightly acidic to neutral pH (6.0 to 7.5). Imbalanced pH can cause nutrient deficiencies or toxicities, affecting crop health and yield.
